A second observation takes this O(2N)O(2^N)O(2N) search spaces into an O(N)O(N)O(N) DP. Visually, the O(KN)O(K^N)O(KN) space is an NNN-dimensional cuboid. Thus, the global minimum must lie at the global minimum of each of the one-dimensional cross-sections, which only need O(KM)O(K^M)O(KM) time to evaluate each.
